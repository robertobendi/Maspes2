# BRIEF

## 1 · Analysis

### What this business actually is
Maspes Piante e Fiori is a third-generation florist in central Como, Italy, in business since 1954. They sell fresh cut flowers, bouquets, indoor plants, orchids, and bespoke arrangements from a single shop on Via Leone Leoni, with delivery across Como and its province. Revenue comes from walk-in shop sales, phone orders, and same-day local delivery — the current Shopify storefront handles transactions but the customer reviews and signature pieces (100-rose arrangements, €700) make clear that the high-value work happens through personal relationship, not anonymous checkout. They are, in effect, a craft atelier with a working storefront, not an e-commerce brand.

### Who the audience really is
- **Primary**: Como-based adults, 30–70, who need flowers for a real occasion right now — a partner's anniversary, a funeral, a dinner-party hostess gift, a hotel lobby, a wedding consultation. They want quality and a human on the phone, not a discount code.
- **Secondary**: Wedding/event planners scouting a fornitore di fiducia, hotels and restaurants in the lake district sourcing regular arrangements, out-of-town buyers sending flowers into Como, and journalists or historians researching a 70-year-old Como business.

### What they're trying to convey
- A historic, family-run craft house — the 1954 founding date and the Maspes surname are the brand's two biggest assets and the current site barely uses either.
- Premium artisanal quality — the price of a 100-rose composition (€700) only makes sense if the presentation signals an atelier, not a shop.
- Local rootedness — they are *the* Como florist, not just *a* Como florist; the reviews ("il fiorista top di Como") prove that's the position they already hold in customer minds.

### What's broken about the current presentation
- The site is a stock Shopify theme with no editorial point of view — generic product grid, no heritage, no atmosphere, no sense of seventy years of work.
- Heritage ("dal 1954") appears once in a page title and nowhere visible on the site — the single most valuable asset is wasted.
- The hero is "Benvenuti / Maspes Piante e Fiori" — a greeting, not a promise; gives the visitor nothing to do.
- Product photography (judging from the catalog structure) is white-background commodity shots; the work itself deserves moody, full-bleed treatment.
- Trust signals are buried — three excellent customer testimonials are stranded near the footer with no visual weight.
- The "palette" extracted from the source is Mastercard/Visa/PayPal payment-button colors — i.e. the site has no brand color of its own, only third-party widget chrome.

### What the rebuild should optimize for
**Primary objective**: get qualified customers to phone the shop or walk in — every page should make the phone number and address feel one tap away, and make the visitor trust that calling Maspes is worth more than tapping "buy" on a faceless site.
Secondary: capture custom-arrangement and wedding inquiries through a single dedicated form; convey the 1954 heritage so that even one-time visitors remember the name.

## 2 · Brand

### Verdict
**Full rebrand.** The current palette is third-party payment-button blue and red, the typography is theme-default, and the visual identity does no work for a 70-year-old atelier with a €700 signature product. The reference images point unambiguously at a dark editorial florist direction — deep forest green, brass accent, full-bleed botanical photography, serif display — and that's where Maspes belongs.

### Palette
- `#1E2A22` — Bosco — primary (deep forest green, dominant surface)
- `#F4ECDD` — Avorio — surface (warm ivory, alternating sections and on-dark text)
- `#B8945A` — Ottone — accent (muted brass, used sparingly for hairlines, CTA fills, monogram)
- `#6B1B1F` — Granata — accent (deep wine, nod to the signature red roses; price tags, ribbons)
- `#11140F` — Carbone — ink (near-black with a green undertone, body text on Avorio)

### Typography
`Heading: Fraunces` + `Body: Inter` — Fraunces gives the editorial-serif warmth the references demand (its optical sizes scale beautifully from a 96px display to a 32px subhead) while Inter keeps Italian body copy, opening hours, and addresses readable and modern; a high-quality florist needs gravitas without becoming a costume.

### Voice
Confident, considered, quietly Italian. Short declarative sentences. Never markety, never breathless — write as if the florist himself is talking to a regular customer he respects. No exclamation marks, no "discover", no "shop now".

### Sample copy
- **Hero headline**: Fiori di Como, dal 1954.
- **Hero subhead**: Tre generazioni di famiglia Maspes a Via Leone Leoni. Rose, orchidee, composizioni su misura — consegna a Como e provincia.
- **Primary CTA**: Chiamaci

### Page content briefs

**Home** (`/`)
Single thing: convince a first-time visitor that Maspes is the most serious florist in Como and put a phone call one tap away.
Sections in order: (1) Hero — full-bleed botanical photograph, display headline, est. 1954 mark, brass "Chiamaci 031 27 11 36" CTA. (2) Heritage intro — short paragraph naming the family, the street, the seventy years. (3) Signature work — three-up editorial cards (Rose · Piante · Bouquet) each linking into Composizioni. (4) Su Misura teaser — one large image + one paragraph + CTA "Richiedi una composizione". (5) Dicono di noi — the three preserved testimonials, set as pull-quotes, no avatars. (6) Visitaci strip — address, today's hours, "Indicazioni" link to Google Maps.
CTAs: phone in header (sticky on mobile), "Chiamaci" in hero, "Richiedi una composizione" in Su Misura teaser, "Indicazioni" in visit strip.
Imagery: hero photograph of a dark, moody bouquet (see imagery plan); three signature-work shots; one wedding/event-style installation shot for Su Misura teaser.

**Composizioni** (`/composizioni`)
Single thing: show the range and quality of what Maspes makes so a visitor either picks up the phone with a specific reference, or just trusts what they see.
Sections: (1) Intro band — short editorial sentence and a filter row (Tutti · Rose · Piante · Bouquet · Orchidee). (2) Asymmetric photo grid of arrangements driven by the new `composizioni` collection — each card is image-led with name, price-from line, and "Chiama per ordinare" link. (3) Closing band — "Non trovi quello che cerchi? Lo facciamo per te." with link to Su Misura.
No checkout, no cart. Every item ends in a phone call.
Imagery: at least 8–12 arrangement photographs; ideally vertical orientation, dark backgrounds, one piece per frame.

**Su Misura** (`/su-misura`)
Single thing: collect a qualified inquiry for bespoke work — weddings, events, recurring B2B, large pieces like the 100-rose composition.
Sections: (1) Editorial hero — "Composizioni su misura" headline + one paragraph naming the use cases (matrimoni, eventi, hotel & ristoranti, regali importanti). (2) Process strip — three steps (Ascoltiamo · Disegnamo · Consegniamo) with one-line description each. (3) Two large reference photographs side by side (a wedding piece, a hotel-style installation). (4) Inquiry form — name, email, phone, occasione (select), data (datetime), budget (select), messaggio (textarea). (5) Or call us — explicit alternative path with phone number.
Form: Formspree placeholder (see Forms below).

**Visitaci** (`/visitaci`)
Single thing: get the visitor through the door, on the right day, at the right time, with confidence.
Sections: (1) Short editorial intro — the family, Corrado, the street, the year. (2) Map block — static Google Maps embed/image of Via Leone Leoni 2. (3) Indirizzo & contatti — clearly typeset address, phone, email, PEC, P.IVA. (4) Orari — full weekly hours table, Avorio surface so it reads cleanly. (5) "Indicazioni" CTA opening Maps app.
No form, no testimonials — keep it practical and uncluttered.
Imagery: one storefront/interior photograph (or a careful Wikimedia substitute of a Como street if no source asset exists), one portrait-style detail of arrangements being made.

### Collections

Default `pages` and `contact` are kept. `posts` is removed — Maspes has no editorial blog; pretending otherwise rots fast.

**`composizioni`** — required (drives the gallery on `/composizioni` and the three-up on `/`)
- route: `/composizioni`, template: `composizione.html`, list_template: `composizioni-list.html`
- fields:
  - `title` (text, required) — name of the arrangement (e.g. "100 Rose Rosse")
  - `slug` (slug, required)
  - `categoria` (select: `rose`, `piante`, `bouquet`, `orchidee`, `composizione`, required) — drives the filter row
  - `prezzo_da` (number, required) — euros, plain integer, displayed as "da €X"
  - `prezzo_fisso` (boolean) — if true, displayed without "da"
  - `immagine_url` (url, required) — primary photo
  - `immagine_alt` (text, required) — italian alt text
  - `descrizione` (textarea, required) — 1–3 sentences, editorial tone
  - `in_evidenza` (boolean) — surfaces it on the homepage three-up
  - `ordine` (number) — manual sort within category
- Justification: the gallery is the entire reason to have a CMS for this site — pages alone would force the owner to edit templates to add an arrangement.

**`testimonianze`** — required
- route: none (used by other templates only), template: none, list_template: none
- fields:
  - `nome` (text, required) — e.g. "Jacopo Zuppati"
  - `citazione` (textarea, required)
  - `mostra_home` (boolean) — three flagged entries appear on home
  - `ordine` (number)
- Justification: three testimonials exist in the source and need to be CMS-managed because the owner will want to add more; not big enough to warrant its own page.

### Forms
**Formspree placeholder.** A serious atelier sending the form result to a real inbox fits the brand better than a `mailto:` that leaks the owner's address. Use `action="https://formspree.io/f/REPLACE_ME"` on the `/su-misura` form, with an HTML comment `<!-- TODO: replace REPLACE_ME with the Formspree endpoint once the account is created. Form posts go to info@maspespiantefiori.it. -->` next to the opening `<form>` tag. Include a hidden `_subject` field set to "Nuova richiesta su misura — maspesfiori.it".

## 4 · Design

### Direction
**Editorial florist atelier — dark, slow, Italian.** A deep forest-green house with full-bleed botanical photography carrying the weight, a single warm brass accent doing all the highlighting, and high-contrast serif display set generously around plenty of negative space. Visual touchstones: **Gilly Flowers** (reference 01 — dark green canvas, large serif, photo-as-half-the-page hero, restrained brass button), **Rosery Flower Shop** (reference 00 — art-deco fan motif, gold arch framing botanicals, editorial section pacing), and **Aesop** product pages for their discipline around type and air. Anti-references: anything that looks like a Shopify theme, anything with rounded full-color cards, anything pastel.

### Type scale
1.25 modular scale, anchored on body 1rem = 16px. Fraunces optical-size axis used: 144 for Display, 72 for H1/H2, 36 for H3, default for everything else.
- **Display** (hero h1): `6.0rem / 1.02 / 400` (Fraunces, opsz 144, slight negative tracking −0.01em)
- **H1**: `4.0rem / 1.08 / 400` (Fraunces, opsz 72)
- **H2**: `2.75rem / 1.15 / 400` (Fraunces, opsz 72)
- **H3**: `1.5rem / 1.3 / 500` (Fraunces, opsz 36)
- **Body**: `1.0625rem / 1.65 / 400` (Inter)
- **Small / meta**: `0.8125rem / 1.5 / 500, tracking 0.12em, uppercase` (Inter — used for eyebrows like "EST. 1954", "POSIZIONE")

### Spacing & rhythm
- Container max-width: **1280px** (with 1120px text-only inner column on prose pages)
- Section vertical padding: small **5rem** (80px) · large **9rem** (144px) — large is the default; small is reserved for adjacent dark-on-dark transitions
- Grid gutters: **2rem** (32px) desktop, **1.25rem** mobile
- No strict baseline grid — Fraunces optical sizes make a fixed baseline fight the type. Maintain rhythm via the spacing tokens above and a consistent `--measure: 65ch` for body prose.

### Components

**Header / nav**
- 88px tall on desktop, 64px mobile, no border, sits over the hero on `/` (transparent on Bosco), solid Bosco on inner pages.
- Layout: left-aligned wordmark "MASPES" in Fraunces small caps with brass est. 1954 line beneath; right cluster: 3 nav links + brass-outlined "Chiamaci 031 27 11 36" button.
- Mobile: wordmark left, hamburger right; menu slides down full-bleed on Bosco with links set in Fraunces 2rem, phone CTA pinned bottom.

**Hero**
- Full viewport-height on `/` (min 720px), 60vh on inner pages.
- Two configurations: (a) **Split** — large vertical botanical photograph occupying ~55% of the width, display headline + subhead + CTA on the remaining Bosco field (used on `/` and `/su-misura`). (b) **Editorial centered** — display headline centered on Bosco with a thin brass underline rule, supporting paragraph and eyebrow label above (used on `/composizioni` and `/visitaci`).
- A 1px brass hairline runs the full width at the bottom of every hero, breaking into a small geometric mark (an arched-window glyph, echoing reference 00) at center.

**Standard content section**
- 50/50 image + text by default, alternating sides per section (left-image, then right-image, then left-image…).
- Text column max 50ch; eyebrow label in brass small-caps above an H2; body paragraph; optional inline link in Ottone with a 1px underline.
- On Avorio surface sections, ink switches to Carbone; on Bosco sections, ink switches to Avorio. No section ever uses both surfaces inside it.

**Card** (composizioni grid)
- Vertical photograph, 3:4 aspect, no rounded corners (sharp edges read serious), 0 padding inside the image.
- Below the image, 1rem vertical gap, then arrangement name in Fraunces 1.5rem, then price line in Inter small-caps 0.8125rem with a brass leading hairline, then "Chiama per ordinare →" link in Ottone.
- Hover: image scales 1.02 over 400ms ease-out; brass arrow shifts 4px right. Card itself does not lift.

**Footer**
- Bosco background, three columns on desktop (Contatti · Orari · Note legali), single column stacked on mobile.
- Dense but quiet: Inter 0.875rem, line-height 1.7, all links Avorio at 80% opacity, Ottone on hover.
- Bottom strip: copyright left, Instagram glyph + Maps glyph right, all 14px.

**Buttons**
- **Primary** — Ottone fill, Carbone text, no border, 0.875rem Inter uppercase tracking 0.12em, 1.25rem × 2.25rem padding, no rounding. Hover: background shifts to Avorio, text stays Carbone; transition 200ms. Focus: 2px Ottone outline offset 4px.
- **Secondary** — Transparent fill, 1px Ottone border, Ottone text. Hover: fills with Ottone, text becomes Carbone.
- **Link** — Ottone text, 1px underline at 0.18em underline-offset. Hover: underline thickens to 2px.

**Forms**
- Inputs: no background fill on Avorio surface (transparent), 1px Carbone-at-30% bottom border only, no top/side borders, 0.75rem vertical padding, Inter 1rem.
- Labels: brass small-caps eyebrow above each field; floating labels are forbidden (they fight the editorial tone).
- Selects styled identically with a custom chevron in Ottone.
- Submit button: primary button style, full-width on mobile, auto on desktop.

### Motion
Very little, slow when used. Fades on scroll-into-view at 600ms ease-out with a 16px upward translate, never more, never longer; image hover scales 1.02 over 400ms; brass arrows shift 4px on hover. No parallax, no carousels, no auto-playing video. The hero photograph never moves — let it carry weight by being still.

### What NOT to do
- Do not use bright saturated red anywhere — Granata only, and only for accent. The "red roses" idea is in the photography, not in the UI chrome.
- Do not build a working cart, "Add to cart" buttons, or fake-checkout flows — every commerce action must end in a phone call. Anything else is dishonest given the static export.
- Do not slap on a green-and-pink "feminine florist" palette — the references reject that direction and so does the brand history.
- Do not put a hero carousel. One photograph, held steady, beats any rotation.
- Do not let the Shopify-style white-background product shot creep back in. Every photograph must be dark-background or full-bleed editorial; better to omit a photo than to use a commodity one.
